About me

Hi! I'm Daniel. I was born and raised in Venezuela where I attended Universidad Del Zulia and graduated with a bachelor's degree in Chemical Engineering in 2015. For my senior project, I designed a recovery pilot plant for automotive used oil, for which my team won the support of a Ford dealership to carry on the project. Also, we were selected to be published in Univeridad Del Zulia scientist magazine, "REDILUZ". During my time in college, I was part of multiple student groups, and attended 2 international student conferences in Costa Rica and Argentina. Upon graduation, I joined a Christian campus ministry and worked there until 2016. Afterward, I moved to the United States, and the past year worked as Quality Technician at the ZF plant in Oakwood, GA.

I've always had a natural curiosity for the "why of things" and it excites me to see the process and outcome of solving problems. Now that I've moved to the States, this curiosity has brought me to Software Engineering. So after a long process of validating my credentials, I was accepted in the Fundamentals of Computer Science Graduate Certificate Program at Kennesaw State University. Once I've finished the certificate program, I will be qualified to apply to the Computer Science master degree at Kennesaw or any other university. In the meantime, I'm glad to be accepted and have joined the Full Stack Web Development Program at Georgia Tech University for the Summer of 2020 with which I aim to get a step ahead in the workplace while pursuing further education.

Moving to the States has been the biggest change I've ever gone through, but it made me realize how resilient one can be and gave me a different confidence in myself. Many positives changes have occurred, such as learning a new language, discovering Mexican food, and meeting my beautiful wife. In my free time, I enjoy DIY projects, all kinds of water activities, and recently I've gotten interested in apiculture.
